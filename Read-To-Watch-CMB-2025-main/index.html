The Fantastic Four: First Steps

The 2025 Fantastic Four movie will introduce the team into the MCU, directed by Matt Shakman. Set in a 1960s-inspired world, it follows Reed Richards (Pedro Pascal), Sue Storm (Vanessa Kirby), Johnny Storm (Joseph Quinn), and Ben Grimm (Ebon Moss-Bachrach) as they face Galactus and his herald, Silver Strong. A key part of the plot is Sue’s pregnancy with their son, Franklin Richards, whose powers play a huge role in the conflict.
The film mixes superhero action with family drama, showing the Fantastic Four balancing their lives as both parents and heroes. The cast also includes Paul Walter Hauser, John Malkovich, Natasha Lyonne, and Sarah Niles. After this, the team will appear in Avengers: Doomsday(2026) and Avengers: Secret Wars (2027).

Comics To Read

The Coming Of Galactus
by Stan Lee & Jack Kirby

The Coming Of Galactus (1966)
Written Stan Lee and Jack Kirby introduces Galactus, a cosmic entity that devours planets. With the help of the Silver Surfer and the Ultimate Nullifier, the Fantastic Four stop Galactus from destroying Earth, expanding the Marvel Universe and solidifying Galactus as a key villain.

The Fantastic Four
by Stan Lee & Jack Kirby

The Fantastic Four (1961)
Written by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, introduced Marvel's first superhero team. After a space mission goes wrong, Reed, Sue, Johnny, and Ben gain powers and become the Fantastic Four. They battle the Mole Man, and soon face iconic villains like Doctor Doom and Galactus. This comic launched Marvel’s interconnected universe and brought real human struggles to superhero stories.

Fantastic Four Vol. VII
by Ryan North

Fantastic Four (2022)
Written by Ryan North focuses on the team's personal stories, highlighting their relationships and growth. With a mix of humor and heart, the series explores smaller adventures, including time loops and a confrontation with Doctor Doom. North’s approach gives the team a fresh, relatable feel, praised for its emotional depth and humor.

Daredevil: Born Again

Charlie Cox is back as Daredevil, facing off against Wilson Fisk (Vincent D’Onofrio), who’s now New York’s mayor. Jon Bernthal returns as The Punisher, promising brutal showdowns.

Daredevil: Born Again launched March 4, 2025, with 9 intense episodes. It leans darker and more grounded, channeling the spirit of the original Netflix series after a major creative reboot.

Season 2 is confirmed for 2026 with Karen Page (Deborah Ann Woll), Foggy Nelson (Elden Henson), and Bullseye (Wilson Bethel) returning — plus new big names like Matthew Lillard. A Punisher-focused special is also in the works!

Daredevil: Born Again is Frank Miller’s story of Matt Murdock losing everything after Kingpin learns his secret. Broken and beaten, Matt finds hope, rebuilds himself, and rises to fight back — a raw, unforgettable story.

THUNDERBOLTS* MAY 2, 2025

Coming May 2, 2025, Thunderbolts brings together a team of antiheroes—Yelena Belova, Bucky Barnes, Red Guardian, and more—forced into a covert mission by Valentina Allegra de Fontaine. It’s a grittier, more emotional Marvel story about redemption, trust, and blurred moral lines.

Directed by Jake Schreier, the film stars Florence Pugh, Sebastian Stan, and Lewis Pullman as the unstable superpowered Sentry. Filmed across several global locations, Thunderbolts is already getting attention for its darker tone and strong performances, with Pugh’s role being a standout.

Comics To Read

Thunderbolts
By Kurt Busiek

Thunderbolts (1997) #1 kicks off with a new superhero team stepping up after the Avengers are gone. They seem like noble heroes—led by Citizen V—but the big twist reveals they’re actually the Masters of Evil in disguise. Written by Kurt Busiek with art by Mark Bagley, this issue is famous for that shocking reveal and sets the stage for one of Marvel’s most unique teams.

Thunderbolts: Dark Reign
By Andy Diggle

Thunderbolts (2006) #132 by Andy Diggle and artist Roberto De La Torre marks a turning point for the team. Following Norman Osborn’s rise to power after Secret Invasion, he reshapes the Thunderbolts into a covert black-ops squad. This issue introduces a new, darker lineup—including characters like Ghost, Paladin, and Ant-Man (Eric O’Grady)—tasked with dirty work the government can’t be linked to. It's gritty, tense, and full of moral gray areas, showcasing the Thunderbolts as ruthless enforcers under Osborn’s control.

Thunderbolts: Revolution
By Collin Kelly & Jackson Lanzing

Thunderbolts (2023) #1 kicks off a new era with Bucky Barnes leading a black-ops team for Valentina Allegra de Fontaine. Alongside Black Widow, U.S. Agent, Red Guardian, and others, they take on secret missions to clean up the darker corners of the Marvel Universe. It’s fast-paced, gritty, and sets the tone for a morally gray, high-stakes series.

IRON HEART

Ironheart, premiering June 24, 2025 on Disney+, follows Riri Williams (Dominique Thorne), a tech genius who returns to Chicago after Black Panther: Wakanda Forever. There, she clashes with Parker Robbins aka The Hood (Anthony Ramos), a villain wielding dark magic. The series explores the clash between science and the supernatural, as Riri balances her brilliance with personal growth.

Created by Chinaka Hodge and produced by Marvel’s Kevin Feige and Ryan Coogler, Ironheart features Lyric Ross, Alden Ehrenreich, and Shea Couleé in supporting roles. Directed by Sam Bailey and Angela Barnes, the 6-episode series continues Riri’s MCU journey and deepens Marvel’s exploration of magic versus technology.

Comics To Read

Ironheart (2018) #1

Ironheart (2018) #1 picks up Riri Williams’ story as she steps out of Tony Stark’s shadow to become her own hero. Now at MIT and building a life beyond the Iron Man legacy, Riri faces new challenges, villains, and questions about who she really wants to be. With heart, brains, and her own unique armor, she’s forging a path that’s all her own.

Invincible Iron Man (2016) #1

Invincible Iron Man (2016) #1 kicks off Riri Williams’ journey as Ironheart, a 15-year-old genius who builds her own suit after Tony Stark steps away. With the help of an AI version of Tony, Riri begins her path as a new kind of hero, blending her smarts with heart. It’s a fresh start for the Iron Man legacy with a focus on diversity and a powerful new character.

Champions (2019) #1

In Champions (2019) #1, Riri Williams teams up with other young heroes like Ms. Marvel and Miles Morales to form a new version of the Champions. As Ironheart, she fights for a better world alongside friends who share her ideals, but leadership, teamwork, and tough moral choices make the journey anything but simple. It’s a bold, energetic start to a new era for Marvel’s next generation.
